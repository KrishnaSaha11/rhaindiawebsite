## What determines rice husk ash price

There is no single list price for RHA — it is quoted per order. The main drivers are:

- **Grade & silica content** — higher, more consistent SiO₂ (85–92%) and lower
  loss on ignition command a higher price because they deliver more reactive material.
- **Form** — [micronized ground powder](/rice-husk-ash-ground-powder/),
  [granules](/rice-husk-ash-granules/) and [pellets](/rice-husk-ash-pellets/) are
  processed differently, so each is priced differently.
- **Order volume / MOQ** — bulk and jumbo-bag quantities lower the per-tonne cost.
- **Packing** — 25/50 kg bags vs jumbo (FIBC) bags vs loose bulk.
- **Destination & Incoterms** — domestic ex-works vs FOB/CIF export pricing, plus
  freight to your port.
- **Documentation** — NABL test reports and export paperwork are included in our
  quotes so there are no surprises.

> Because these vary by order, a tailored quote is almost always cheaper and more
> accurate than a generic "list" price.

## How RHA is priced for bulk & export

For export buyers we quote on an FOB or CIF basis to your destination port,
typically in metric tonnes, with the grade, packing and lead time spelled out.
For domestic orders we quote ex-works Sambalpur, Odisha, or delivered. Every
quote states the indicative specification and confirms that NABL lab values are
available on request.
